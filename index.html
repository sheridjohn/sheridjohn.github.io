About Me

My name is John Sheridan, and I have created this website to practice and improve upon my web developer skillset. After obtaining my degree in computer applications from DCU, I had intended to start my career along the software development track. However, this did not exactly work out as intended. After graduating from college I went to work for the company in which I had completed a work placement with during college. My role there was a mix between development and support. I moved to a developer suppot role in a larger company after 9 months. The developer support role appealed to me as I believed it would be a good way to get to know the product through the support aspect of the job, and also hone my development skills there, with many development projects planned for the team to work on. Though I did work on some development projects, the company's leadership transitioned the team from development support to application support. Which, within that company, removed the opportnity to gain more experience in this regard.

From there, I stayed on the support track for my next few roles, as I enjoyed many aspects of the support role, and had developed a strong skill set within this field. However, I found that potential employers were initially eager for my programming background, and offered me the opportunity to work on development projects and building support tooling during the interview process, but ultimately the role never led to these projects. While I love the problem solving and troubleshooting of support, I find that I do not get quite the same satisfaction from this alone, as I do from working on code. In late February 2020 I found the application Mimo and started to go through the web developer course. It reminded me how much I love writing, running, and debugging code. There is no satisfaction for me quite like working on code and seeing it come to life. I decided to resign from my support job and spend some time improving the foundations of my web development knowledge, with the intention of transferring to an entry or junior level web developer role, or a support role where I can use and build upon my developer skillset.

It is a bit of a risk, but ultimately I was always wondering if I was capable of a role within development. I do not want to have any regrets or be left wondering about a career in software development, so I've decided to take the leap and try my hand at it.

About this Site

I've built this site to show I have a grasp of the fundamentals of front end web development: HTML, CSS, and Javascript. There is a page for my C.V, a page for some javascript exercises and samples, a page where the user can get in touch with me, and this About page. As well as the page for javascript examples I've also coded a button which can be used to toggle the site between light and dark mode, which you can click on the bottom of each page. I'm also curently building a page to show my understanding and skills in developing with React, but this is a work in progress at the moment. As the site progresses I'm hoping to also add in some back end functionality as well.
