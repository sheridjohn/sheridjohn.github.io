Becoming a web developer

After getting my degree in computer applications from DCU, I had intended to start my career along the development track. However, after graduating from college I went back to the company I had done my placement with during college. My role there was a mix between development and support. I moved to a developer suppot role in a bigger company after that. This role appealed to me as I believed it would be a good way to get to know the product through the support aspect of the job, and also hone my development skills there. Though I did get some dev projects to work on there, the companys leadership transitioned the team from development support to application support. Which meant there was very little time to be given to dev projects, as these would be totally outside the expected day to day responsibilities of the role.

From there, I stayed on the support track for my next few jobs, as I did enjoy, and was good at, many aspects of the support role. However, I never really got the same satisfaction from that as I did from writing code and seeing it run. In late February 2020 I found the application Mimo and started to go through the web developer course. It really reminded me how much I love writing, running, and debugging code. There's no satisfaction for me quite like working on code and seeing it come to life. So, as I and my partner had some savings to cover my share of the mortgage for a few months, I decided to resign from my support job and spend some time going over the basics of front end web development, in order to get an entry or junior level front end/web developer role.
